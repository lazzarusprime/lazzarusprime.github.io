What is a Cavernous Malformation?

Cavernous malformations (also known as cavernomas or cavernous angiomas) are clusters of abnormal, dilated blood vessels usually located in the brain or spinal cord. These clusters resemble mulberries and have extremely thin, weak walls.

Because the blood vessel walls are so thin, they can leak or hemorrhage into surrounding tissue. This can cause a wide variety of serious symptoms depending on the location and severity of the bleed, including seizures, severe headaches, vision or hearing issues, loss of balance, or neurological deficits like weakness and numbness.
